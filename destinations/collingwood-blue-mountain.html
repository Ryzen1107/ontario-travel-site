Collingwood
Blue Mountain Escape

A four-season weekend that can be active or relaxed: Blue Mountain Village, Georgian Bay, Scandinave Spa, scenic lookouts, Thornbury, cider, beaches, winter skiing, and a polished resort-town feel without flying anywhere.

Why it is worth the drive

The easiest all-season resort weekend

Collingwood / Blue Mountain is not the wildest Ontario trip, but it is one of the easiest weekends to execute well.

You can make it adventurous with skiing, hiking, biking, zipline-style activities, and Scenic Caves; or you can make it romantic with Scandinave Spa, dinner in Collingwood, a village walk, and Georgian Bay sunsets.

The key is not to spend the entire weekend inside the Village. Use Blue Mountain as one anchor, then add Collingwood, Thornbury, Georgian Bay, and a slow scenic drive.

Planning rule: treat Blue Mountain Village as the base, not the whole trip. Pair it with spa, Georgian Bay, or Thornbury for a better weekend.

Where to Sleep

Stay Strategy

Value-first, premium only if memorable

Under $200: Collingwood motel / weekday deals

Best value is usually outside the Village, especially midweek or off-season.

$200–350: village condo / nearby inn

Good for walkability and convenience if you plan to spend time at Blue.

$350–500: Westin / premium village stay

Worth it when you want ease, walkability, and a special-but-not-too-far trip.

Rare splurge: spa + premium stay combo

The splurge should be the full experience: nice room + Scandinave + dinner.

Why it is worth the drive

The experience in one honest view

Prince Edward County is not about one single attraction. It is about the feel: country roads, vineyards, old towns, beaches, farm stands, small restaurants, and a weekend that moves slowly. For a couple trip, PEC works best when you pick one base and do short loops from there.

This is one of the best places for a relaxed summer or early fall weekend, especially if you combine Sandbanks, one winery, one great meal, and one lakeside sunset.

Planning philosophy: This page is built for slow travel from Scarborough — one main area, fewer stops, better memories.

What to actually do

Top Experiences

Prioritized for a couple trip

1. Sandbanks Provincial Park

Huge freshwater dunes, beaches, warm shallow summer water, and the most iconic nature stop in the County. Book day-use early in peak season.

Driving plan

Route From Scarborough

0:00

Leave Scarborough late morning

PEC does not require a punishing early start unless you have Sandbanks parking for a specific time.

2:45

Arrive Picton / Wellington

Choose a base first. Do not keep crossing the County repeatedly.

3:15

One afternoon anchor

Either Sandbanks beach or one winery + dinner. Not both rushed.

Food planning

Food Guide

Dinner

Book ahead in Wellington or Picton during summer weekends.

Vegetarian Strategy

PEC is usually better than many small-town destinations for vegetarian-friendly menus, but still check before booking.
